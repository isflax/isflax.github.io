Teshen Silesian (or NE Lach) is a transitional Czech-Polish dialect or rather a special West Slavic language which is still spoken in the territory of SE Silesian counties Karvin· and Fr˝dek-MÌstek by a great number of their country inhabitants (about 150,000 native speakers). This living Lechitic language has not any official written form yet, and so I've decided to improve it, to change it... Teshen Silesian is also called Çpo naöymuë among its native speakers.

ALPHABET

Teshen Silesian alphabet and orthography is almost fully phonetical, being such a combination of Czech, Polish & Sorbian.

IPA PHONEMES

Teshen Silesian vowels are similar to Polish: Only the Çaë and Çuë exist also in Czech. The Çië is much higher and softer than Czech Çië & Çyë; the Çeë is a little closer than Czech mid front unrounded Çeë; the Çoë is more open and rounded than Czech Çoë (the same as North Germanic Ça-circleë or Ç‚ë in F‚rsi); the Ço-breveë is an extremely rounded Çoë (Polish ÇÛë, Swedish Çoë) which is somewhere between the Çoë and Çuë in Czech. Teshen Silesian Çyë is hard as in Polish. The ÇÈë grapheme represents close central Çeë that is pronounced somewhere between the close and close-mid central unrounded vowel, and its sound is very similar to Çyë but these phonemes are not the same. The ÇÈë is used just a seldom. All these sounds are short as in Polish. Phonetically, there are eight vowel sounds in Teshen Silesian.

Teshen Silesian consonants are almost the same as in Polish language. The Ç¯ë is pronounced somewhere between Czech Ç¯ë (voiced fricative alveolar trill) and Polish Çrzë (voiced retroflex fricative). The Çhë is not voiced as in Czech (not so strong) but it sounds like the Çhë in English Çheadë. Letters "Ë, dû, ö, û" are pronounced like Polish "cz, dø, sz and ø" (being a little harder - more retroflex - than in Czech or in English). The Çwë is used very seldom in some loanwords (e.g. in Çwolframë).
